Overview

  • ended on July 3, 1969
  • started after a police raid at Stonewall Inn, Lower Manhattan
  • start of the modern LQBTQ+ movement
  • June became the global gay-pride month in honor of stonewall
  • Series of Riots and Protests by Queer people against police repression and homophopic legislation
  • they began in the early morning hours of June 28, 1969
  • ended on July 3, 1969
  • started after a police raid at the Stonewall Inn in the Christopher street, in Lower Manhattan, New York City

  • June became the global gay-pride month in honor of stonewall, in larger western citys -> pride marches / Christopher Street day

The Riots

only known picture of the first night of the riots

the first night

  • 4 undercover police officers were in the Stonewall Inn
  • at 1:20am, 8 more officers entered the inn and began the raid
  • cross-dressers and trans women were arrested, the identity of others was checked
  • Stormé DeLarverie escaped the police and shouted: "Why don't you guys do something?" as she was put in a police van
  • Stormé DeLarverie: lesbian singer, sometimes called the rose parks of the gay liberation movement
  • Patrons tried to escape the Inn

Violence breaks out

  • the crowd outside the Stonewall Inn became violent
  • they slashed a few tires of police vehicles
  • the crowd threw rocks and bottles at the officers
  • Garbage Cans were lit
  • Police fled to the inside to protect themselves

Sylvia Rivera was a gay liberation and transgender rights activist. She was a drag queen and participated in demonstrations with the Gay Liberation Front. The Gay Liberation Front was formed immediately after the Riots and organized demonstrations for gay rights
